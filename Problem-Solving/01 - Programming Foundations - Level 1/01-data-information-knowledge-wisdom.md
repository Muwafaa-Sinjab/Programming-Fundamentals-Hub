# Data, Information, Knowledge, and Wisdom

## Data

Raw facts with no structure and no context. On their own, numbers or symbols like this carry no meaning and give no direct benefit.

Example: a list of temperature readings recorded every day for 100 years is just a stream of numbers.

## Information

Data becomes information once it is organized and placed in context. Information answers direct questions such as who, what, where, or when.

Example: calculating from the temperature data that the average rises by one degree every ten years turns raw numbers into a meaningful statement.

## Knowledge

Knowledge comes from connecting many pieces of information over time. It explains *how* and *why* something happens, and it can be used to predict outcomes.

Example: linking rising temperatures to carbon emissions produces an understanding of climate change, not just a description of it.

## Wisdom

Wisdom is the ability to act on knowledge responsibly, weighing long-term and ethical consequences before making a decision.

## Full Example Chain

| Level       | Example                                                                 |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data        | 10,000 births recorded per day                                          |
| Information | Population doubles every year                                           |
| Knowledge   | Fast population growth will overwhelm resources and infrastructure      |
| Wisdom      | Build a long-term plan to expand resources before a crisis happens      |

## Key Takeaway

Each level depends on the one before it: data is organized into information, information accumulates into knowledge, and knowledge is applied through wisdom to make sound decisions.
