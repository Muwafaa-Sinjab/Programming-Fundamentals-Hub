# 12 - ISP (Internet Service Provider)

## Overview

This video explains the role of an Internet Service Provider (ISP). It covers what an ISP is, how it connects local networks to the global internet, and why users pay for these services even though no single entity owns the internet.

## Main Topics

### What is an ISP?

ISP stands for Internet Service Provider. It is the company responsible for providing internet access to homes and businesses. Examples of ISPs include national telecommunication companies that offer internet subscriptions to individual users and organizations.

### The Role of the ISP

The primary function of an ISP is to connect a local network to the global internet. When a home or company sets up a router, that router only creates a local network. Without an ISP, the devices on that local network cannot reach the outside world. The ISP assigns a public IP address to the connection and provides a specific data speed based on the user's subscription plan.

### The Connection Path

Data travels from local devices to the local router, and then from the router to the ISP. The ISP acts as the gateway or portal that forwards this data to the global internet. This means the ISP is the bridge between a private local network and the rest of the world.

### Why We Pay for Internet Access

A common question is why users must pay for internet access if nobody actually owns the internet. While the internet itself is a global network of networks that no single person or company controls, reaching it requires massive physical infrastructure. This infrastructure includes underground and underwater cables, large data centers, and powerful routers. Users do not pay for the internet itself, but rather for the infrastructure, maintenance, and service the ISP provides to connect their local network to the global network. Free applications and websites are only accessible because the ISP provides the physical connection required to reach them.

## Key Takeaways

- ISP stands for Internet Service Provider
- It provides homes and businesses with access to the global internet
- An ISP assigns a public IP address and a specific connection speed to the user
- The ISP acts as the bridge between a private local network and the global internet
- Users pay ISPs for the physical infrastructure and service required to access the internet, not for the internet itself
