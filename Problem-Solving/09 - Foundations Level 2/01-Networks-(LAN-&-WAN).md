# 01 - Networks (LAN & WAN)

## Overview

This video introduces the basic concepts of computer networks. It explains what a network is, the main types of networks, how devices connect to each other, and the role of protocols in network communication.

## Main Topics

### What is a Network?

A network is a group of devices connected to each other. These devices are not limited to computers; they can include mobile phones, tablets, printers, or any smart home devices connected through WiFi. The main purpose of a network is to enable communication and data sharing between connected devices. Practical uses include tracking devices in a pharmacy, sharing files between devices, and easily adding new devices to an existing network.

### Types of Networks

There are two main types of networks:

- **LAN (Local Area Network)**: A network that covers a small area. It can be as small as a home network or as large as a university or company network. A large organization may have more than one LAN network within its premises.

- **WAN (Wide Area Network)**: A network that connects multiple LAN networks together. It spans across cities, countries, or even continents and can contain thousands or millions of connected devices. The Internet itself is the largest example of a WAN. An important distinction is that a WAN is not simply a network of devices, but rather a network of connected LAN networks.

### Connection Methods

Devices on a network can connect in two ways:

- **Wired Connection**: Devices connect using physical cables. The cables connect to a device called a switch. The set of rules that organizes this type of connection is called Ethernet.

- **Wireless Connection**: Devices connect without cables using radio signals. This method is commonly known as WiFi, which stands for Wireless Fidelity. WiFi is slower and has a shorter range compared to Ethernet, but it is easier to use because it does not require physical cables.

### Protocols

A protocol is a set of rules that determines how devices communicate with each other, whether through wired or wireless connections. Ethernet is the protocol used for wired communication, while WiFi is the primary method for wireless communication. Both serve the same purpose of enabling data transfer, but they differ in speed, range, and convenience.

## Key Takeaways

- A network is a group of connected devices that communicate and share data
- LAN covers a small area like a home or office, while WAN connects multiple LANs across large distances
- The Internet is the largest example of a WAN
- Devices can connect through wired cables (Ethernet) or wireless signals (WiFi)
- Protocols are the rules that govern how devices communicate on a network
