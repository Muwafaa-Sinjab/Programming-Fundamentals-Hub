# 02 - MAN Network

## Overview

This video introduces the Metropolitan Area Network (MAN). It explains where this type of network fits in terms of size, the geographic areas it typically covers, and how it compares to LAN and WAN networks in terms of performance, cost, and complexity.

## Main Topics

### What is MAN?

MAN stands for Metropolitan Area Network. It is a type of network that is larger than a Local Area Network (LAN) but smaller than a Wide Area Network (WAN). A MAN generally covers a geographic area such as a single city, a governorate, or a group of connected villages.

### Network Structure

Similar to how a WAN is formed by connecting multiple LAN networks, a MAN is also built by connecting several LANs together. The difference is the geographic scope. While a WAN connects LANs across countries or continents, a MAN connects LANs that are located within the same city or metropolitan region.

### Comparison with LAN and WAN

Because a MAN falls between a LAN and a WAN in size, its technical characteristics also fall between the two. A MAN provides faster data speeds than a WAN, but slower speeds than a LAN. It experiences less signal noise and interference compared to a WAN, but more than a LAN. Additionally, the cost to set up and maintain a MAN is higher than a LAN but lower than a WAN, and its overall complexity is lower than a WAN.

## Key Takeaways

- MAN stands for Metropolitan Area Network
- It covers a larger area than a LAN but a smaller area than a WAN
- It typically spans a single city, governorate, or group of villages
- Like a WAN, a MAN is built by connecting multiple LAN networks together
- Its speed, cost, and complexity level fall between those of LAN and WAN
